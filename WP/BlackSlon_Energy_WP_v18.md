## PART I - WHY NOW

### 1. Energy as the Settlement Currency of the Future

#### 1.1 The End of Fiat as the Ultimate Reference
Every major financial system in history has required an ultimate reference - a unit of value that exists independently of political will. Gold was that reference for centuries: scarce, physical, universally recognised, impossible to print. The Bretton Woods collapse in 1971 severed that anchor. Since then, the global economy has operated on fiat currencies whose value rests entirely on institutional trust and governmental decree.

That system is showing its structural limits. Fiat currencies can be issued without physical constraint. Their value is determined by committee, by election cycle, by geopolitical pressure. They are, at their core, a promise - and promises are only as durable as the institutions that make them.

A kilowatt-hour of energy is not a promise. It is a physical constant. The same unit of energy that powers a steel mill in Wodowa powers a data centre in Dublin and a brewery in Prague. It does not fluctuate based on the creditworthiness of its issuer. It cannot be printed. It can only be extracted, converted, and consumed. Its scarcity is not institutional - it is thermodynamic.

This is the foundation of the BlackSlon thesis: energy is already functioning as the settlement currency of the real economy. The financial system has not yet built the instruments to reflect that reality.

#### 1.2 The Inverted Bitcoin
Bitcoin is the most important monetary experiment of the 21st century - a digital asset whose value is anchored, indirectly but structurally, to the cost of energy. To produce a Bitcoin, you must consume electricity. The more expensive energy becomes, the more costly it is to mine, the scarcer new supply becomes. Bitcoin rises with energy prices.

This relationship is real. It is also incomplete. Bitcoin is a virtual asset. Its connection to energy is mediated - it consumes energy as an input to a computational process, but it does not represent energy itself. You cannot redeem a Bitcoin for a megawatt-hour. The energy relationship is a cost structure, not a claim.

BlackSlon inverts this architecture entirely.

| Bitcoin | BlackSlon Energy Tokens |
|---------|-------------------------|
| Relationship to energy | Consumes energy as input | IS energy - represents 100 kWh directly |
| Value driver | Rises with energy prices (indirect) | Anchored to physical energy markets (direct) |
| Physical redeemability | None | Conditional on V2P activation per market |
| Scarcity mechanism | Algorithmic supply cap | Physical scarcity of energy itself |
| Expiry | None | None - perpetual |
| Geographic specificity | Global, undifferentiated | Local - European power and gas hubs |

Bitcoin is digital proof of energy expenditure. BlackSlon is digital representation of energy value itself. The distinction is not semantic. It is the difference between a receipt for a consumed meal and the meal itself.

#### 1.3 The First Instrument That Bridges Two Worlds
Here is the structural reality of energy commodity markets that has defined them since their inception: the financial world and the physical world have always been permanently separate.

A gasoil forward contract cannot be taken to a petrol station and converted into diesel in your tank. The contract tracks the price of diesel. It is not diesel. The financial exposure and the physical commodity exist in parallel universes - connected by price, separated by everything else: clearing infrastructure, delivery logistics, exchange membership, bilateral legal agreements, and institutional gatekeeping that no retail participant can navigate.

This separation has been accepted as an immutable feature of commodity markets. It is not. It is an architectural choice - one that was never challenged because no instrument existed to challenge it.

BlackSlon introduces a new architecture: Financial On-Chain Physical.

A participant buys BS-P or BS-G tokens on the Order Book. The tokens represent energy value on-chain - anchored to European wholesale hubs, held perpetually, tradeable 24/7. When the participant is ready to consume that value, they transfer tokens to a licensed energy retailer. The retailer delivers kilowatt-hours to their meter. The invoice shows zero for energy. The on-chain position becomes physical energy - without a delivery point obligation, without a bilateral contract, without institutional gatekeeping.

The reverse flow is equally possible: Physical On-Chain Financial. A producer or industrial consumer with physical exposure can tokenise their position - converting it into a liquid, transferable on-chain instrument that can be traded, hedged, or held without the constraints of a physical supply agreement.

For the first time, the boundary between the financial energy market and the physical energy market is not a wall. It is a gateway - and the token is the key.

The underlying is not an algorithm. It is a kilowatt-hour - and unlike block space, kilowatt-hours cannot be printed.

#### 1.5 Why Power and Natural Gas - The Two Tokens of European Energy
The BlackSlon Protocol launches with two energy tokens: BS-P for electric power and BS-G for natural gas. This is not an arbitrary choice. It reflects the structure of the European energy economy - and a unique property that makes these two carriers unlike any other commodity on the continent.

The scale is without parallel. European power markets represent approximately 3,570,000,000 MWh traded annually - a physical delivery value of roughly €250 billion at average 70EUR/MWh wholesale prices. European natural gas markets represent approximately 5,100,000,000 MWh equivalent annually - another €250 billion in physical value (at 50EUR/MWh). Combined physical delivery: approximately 500 billion per year. The financial layer - hedges, spreads, rollovers, speculative flow - multiplies this several times over, placing total notional turnover well into the trillions annually.

They are structurally different - and structurally complementary. Electricity is produced domestically across Europe - from nuclear, renewables, hydro, and gas-fired generation - in a continuous balancing act between production and consumption that cannot tolerate imbalance for more than seconds. It cannot be stored at scale. It is the most local of all commodities.

Natural gas is the opposite: the European Union imports virtually 100% of its consumption through pipelines and LNG terminals. Gas can be stored. It can be transported globally. It is the commodity that links European energy prices to global geopolitics more directly than any other. A glass manufacturer in Liberec, a ceramics producer in Sassuolo, a fertiliser plant in Pulawy - all of them depend on gas not as one input among many, but as the foundational cost that determines whether production is profitable or not. When TTF moves, their margins move with it. When TTF doubles in eight days - as it did following the Iran 2026 conflict - some of them simply stop production.

Together, power and gas define the European energy balance: power sets the moment-to-moment cost of everything the economy produces; gas closes the balancing equation when renewables fall short and sets the marginal price of power generation through gas-fired plants. When natural gas moves, power follows. They are not independent markets. They are the two faces of the same energy economy.

Gas is the geopolitical exposure. The Russian invasion of Ukraine in 2022 was, at its core, an energy war - and natural gas was its primary weapon. The termination of Gazprom's long-term supply contracts, which had provided over 40 percent of all EU gas imports, triggered the largest energy price shock in European history. The Iran 2026 conflict moved TTF by +114.8% in eight trading days. Norwegian export discussions, LNG terminal capacity, Algerian pipeline politics - these are the variables that determine European economic sovereignty. Holding BS-G tokens is not merely a hedge against energy price volatility. It is exposure to the commodity that has proven, repeatedly, to be the most geopolitically consequential asset in Europe.

One unit for One market. A continent-wide standard. Since 2014, both electric power and natural gas in European markets are priced in the same unit: the kilowatt-hour - or its wholesale equivalent, the megawatt-hour. This is unique globally. Singapore is the only other major market with comparable unit standardisation. It means that a BS-P token and a BS-G token share the same unit of account, enabling direct comparison, conversion, and cross-commodity strategy within a single protocol, in a single interface, without currency conversion or unit transformation.

One hundred kilowatt-hours. One token. Every market.

### 2. The Failures BlackSlon Is Solving

Still ten years ago, European energy markets were boring. Prices stable. Annual volatility 2-3 percent. Interest rates negative in Germany - the cost of capital practically zero. A fixed-price annual contract with a supplier: sign it, forget it, pay the invoice. The glass manufacturer in Liberec knew what his gas would cost next year. The bakery in Pozna knew what her electricity bill would be in January.

That world no longer exists.

Since 2020, European energy and gas prices have risen by several hundred percent. In Poland, power traded at 140 PLN/MWh in 2020 - today it trades above 500 PLN/MWh. Germany, Czech Republic, Italy - similar trajectories. Annual volatility now exceeds 50 percent - a level once reserved for cryptocurrencies and speculative technology stocks, not the gas that heats a factory or powers a production line. The cost of money: once negative in the eurozone, today several to more than ten percent annually. A company that wanted to financially hedge its energy exposure now ties up expensive capital for a long period - capital that costs multiples more than it did five years ago.

This is not a business cycle. It is a structural transformation of European energy markets - permanent, deepening, and without instruments for those who do not have 3 million in bank guarantees.

This trillion-euro market is effectively controlled by fewer than 200 entities. ICE Futures Europe in London counts 316 direct participants: Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Shell, BP, TotalEnergies, RWE, Citadel, Optiver. Consolidate to real holding groups and the number falls to approximately 200 unique institutional actors - with the majority of volume concentrated in the hands of perhaps 50.

For the entire remainder of the European economy - every household, every SME, every mid-size industrial consumer, every energy professional outside the institutional stratum - the door has always been closed. The failures that keep it closed are structural, not incidental.

**The Capital Barrier.** Entry requires 3-5 million in liquid capital or bank guarantees before a single trade is executed. Individual bank guarantees are required for every OTC counterparty just to receive a price quote - locking up millions for the privilege of seeing a number on a screen. State-backed giants operate on 30-45 day deferred payment terms while demanding 100 percent prepayment from new entrants.

**The Collapse of Risk Management.** The Iran 2026 conflict moved TTF +114.8% in eight trading days - from 31.96/MWh on February 27th to 68.63/MWh on March 9th - before crashing -19.5% in a single session. The summer of 2022 saw German Cal+1 power surge from approximately 100/MWh to over 900/MWh - not because of a physical shortage, but because the risk management failure of a single large participant cascaded through clearing infrastructure designed to amplify stress, not absorb it. A ceramics producer who had not hedged watched his energy cost multiply tenfold in weeks. A fertiliser plant shut down because producing at spot gas prices was less costly than selling the output.

**The Retail Consumer Trap.** A household or SME customer signs a supply contract at today's retail price. Their supplier procures energy on the wholesale market using instruments the customer cannot access. The structural backwardation discount - the gap between today's spot price and the two-year forward price, typically 10-20 percent - flows entirely into the supplier's margin. Zero forward price protection. Zero hedging tools. Zero portability. When the contract ends, the customer renegotiates from zero at whatever price is available that day.

**Legal and Regulatory Gatekeeping.** Exchange onboarding takes 6-12 months. A single EFET framework agreement costs up to 50,000 in legal fees for a document that offers almost no room for negotiation. This is not compliance. It is a manufactured entry fee designed to maintain exclusivity.

**Counterparty and Systemic Risk.** Over 30 energy suppliers collapsed in the UK since 2020. Gazprom terminated long-term contracts supplying over 40 percent of EU gas imports in 2022. The clearing infrastructure does not absorb crises. It accelerates them - transferring wealth from smaller participants to institutions with unconstrained capital at the exact moment of maximum market stress.

BlackSlon does not add layers of complexity. It strips them away.

**Zero Expiry. Zero Barriers. Zero Counterparty Risk. 100 kWh in one token.**

### 3. The BlackSlon Answer - Zero Form

In 1915, Kazimierz Malewicz abandoned everything he had built within the established movements of his time and exhibited the Black Square - stripped to its irreducible zero. The critics called it the end of painting. It was its reinvention. Malewicz did not simplify. He removed every layer that was not essential.

BlackSlon applies the same principle to European energy markets.

**Zero Expiry. Universal Access.**
BlackSlon reduces the minimum entry threshold by four orders of magnitude. Instead of 744,600 for the smallest German yearly power contract at wholesale market - 1 MW Baseload × 8,760 hours × 85/MWh - participation begins at 100 kWh, a few euros. The same market, accessible to any participant, anywhere, at any time. And once you enter - there is no forced roll, no year-end liquidity crunch, no expiry mechanics. A position opened today remains valid indefinitely, continuously anchored to physical market reality. No maintenance fees. No overnight funding fees. No currency conversion fees.

**Zero Destination Clauses.**
Most gas and power supply agreements carry strict physical delivery clauses. Whether you are QatarGas signing a 15-year LNG offtake or a regional glass manufacturer locking in a three-year gas supply contract - you do not own the price. When energy prices rally and your position is deeply in-the-money, that value is structurally trapped. It is tied to a specific geographic delivery point. You cannot monetise it. You cannot transfer it.

BlackSlon breaks this trap. BS-G and BS-P tokens provide price exposure without delivery point, without volume obligation, without counterparty credit risk. For the first time, the economic value of an energy market position becomes liquid - transferable, tradeable, redeemable - without unwinding the underlying exposure.

**The Democratisation of the Forward Curve.**
When you buy 100 kWh of gas token - BS-G-NL - for a few euros, you are not buying spot exposure. You are holding a token whose pricing corridor already contains the structural discount of the forward curve - the same economic advantage that energy institutions extract through multi-million euro Cal+1 and Cal+2 positions - compressed into a single token, accessible without a credit rating, without a bank guarantee, and without a six-month onboarding process. The protocol rolls continuously into the cheapest part of the forward curve, mechanically and without friction, through the ADR mechanism embedded in the Settlement Anchor. For the first time, the cheapest part of the energy market is also the most accessible.

**Algorithmic Price Benchmark - BSEI.**
Unlike hub benchmarks that rely on end-of-day fixings - which lag real market conditions and create manipulation windows at the close - the BSEI is calculated continuously from executed Order Book transactions using a three-tier Segmented R-VWAP over a 72-hour window. The most recent 24 hours carry 50 percent of the weight, the preceding 24-48 hours carry 25 percent, and the oldest 48-72 hours carry the remaining 25 percent. To manipulate the BSEI, a single actor would need to dominate trading volume across three consecutive days - making single-session manipulation economically unviable.

**Governed Price Discovery - BSSZ.**
The BlackSlon Settlement Zone does not suppress price discovery - it defines the corridor within which BS-P and BS-G token prices move freely through supply and demand. The corridor is anchored daily to the Settlement Anchor - a weighted basket of forward curve segments derived from physical hub prices. The Anchor defines where the zone sits. What happens inside it is determined entirely by the market.

When TTF surged +114.8% in eight trading days following the Iran 2026 conflict and crashed -19.5% in a single session, the BlackSlon Settlement Anchor captured the full directional trend - +55% over the same period, maximum single-day move +19%, zero transmission of the -19.5% collapse. The trend was captured. The noise was not.

**Instant Settlement. No Intermediaries.**
eEURO - a MiCA-compliant euro stablecoin - settles positions in seconds. No clearing banks. No 24-48 hour wire transfer delays. No positions force-closed because a payment arrived one hour late. Capital is liquid, on-chain, and always accessible.

**Institutional-Grade Risk Architecture.**
The protocol operates a dual-layer risk framework: individual account health monitored in real time through the Health Factor (H_user), and systemic protocol solvency tracked through the Protocol Solvency Index (H_solv). Smart Incremental Liquidation replaces catastrophic forced closures with surgical ten-percent position reductions that protect both participants and the Protocol Vault simultaneously.

**Physical Market Tether.**
BlackSlon is not a purely synthetic protocol. The BSSZ corridor - anchored daily to hub settlement prices (TTF, Phelix, NBP, PSV, and expanding) - ensures that virtual token prices operate within bounds that physical market reality defines. In Phase 2, BS-P and BS-G tokens become redeemable for physical energy delivery through integrated licensed retailers. The virtual and physical markets are not separated - they are the same market, accessed through different interfaces.

#### Two Fundamental Classes
Every participant arrives with one of two fundamentally different objectives. There is no registration, no flag, no separate account type. The distinction exists in how participants use the protocol - not in how the protocol treats them. ISA identifies profile through onboarding and guides each class toward the decisions that naturally serve their objectives.

**Price Seekers** seek financial exposure to European energy prices - directional, hedging, or long-term investment. They include professional energy traders taking short-term directional positions, investors holding BS tokens for months or years as an inflation hedge or commodity allocation, arbitrageurs exploiting cross-hub price differences, and financial hedgers offsetting energy price exposure held elsewhere.

Price Seekers create the market. Traders provide continuous Order Book depth. Investors bring long-duration capital that stabilises price discovery. Arbitrageurs keep cross-hub prices aligned. Together they ensure that when a Volume Hedger places an order, there is a real counterparty on the other side at a fair price.

**Volume Hedgers** seek certainty - of price and of supply volume - over a defined forward horizon of 24 to 36 months. They include households wanting to protect themselves against rising energy prices, SMEs with predictable monthly energy consumption, industrial facilities planning multi-year operational budgets, and multi-site manufacturers managing cross-country energy exposure.

For a Volume Hedger, a BS-P or BS-G token is not primarily a financial instrument. It is a forward commitment to an energy price. They accumulate tokens systematically over months, building an average cost below the current market price, and use them to settle their energy supply obligations with their retailer month by month.

The protocol serves both classes simultaneously. Price Seekers provide liquidity. Volume Hedgers provide long-duration, predictable demand. They are the two sides of the same market.

The same token. Three completely different uses. No traditional energy instrument offers even one of them without restrictions.

### 7. The Accumulation Strategy - Buy the Dip on Energy

### 8. The Retailer Model - Coming With Their Own Energy

The conventional energy supply relationship has a fixed structure: the retailer controls the price, the timing, and the information. The customer receives a quote and accepts or walks away.

The BlackSlon model inverts this relationship entirely.

A Volume Hedger does not approach a retailer to ask for a price. They arrive already holding tokens - energy value accumulated over months at prices they chose. The supply agreement is not a price-setting exercise. It is a delivery arrangement.

The retailer does not know the customer's average token acquisition cost. That information is private to the customer and is not disclosed by the protocol. The retailer prices the supply agreement against the current market price on the day the contract is signed. The customer, having accumulated tokens at an average cost below the current market price through patient accumulation, has negotiating leverage the retailer cannot see and cannot counter. The information asymmetry that has always worked against the energy consumer now works in their favour.

For the retailer, the economics are compelling independently of this asymmetry. European wholesale energy markets are structurally in backwardation. A retailer who hedges its procurement obligation against the two-year forward curve and prices its token-settled supply agreement at the same anchor captures the carry between the forward and spot as structural margin - locked in at contract inception, protected from spot volatility for the full term.

A retailer hedging at 38 EUR/MWh on the Cal+2 curve and pricing its token-settled supply agreement at 40 EUR/MWh earns 2 EUR/MWh on every megawatt-hour delivered - certain from day zero. Across a 2,400 MWh two-year gas supply agreement, that is 4,800 EUR in structural margin before collateral yield or protocol fee share. On a portfolio of hundreds of such agreements, the economics are transformative.

More significant than the margin is what disappears from the retailer's risk profile. Under a conventional fixed-price supply agreement, the retailer carries payment default risk for its entire duration. Under the BlackSlon model, every future token transfer is backed by collateral locked in the protocol from the day the supply agreement is signed. The protocol's Proof of Reserve confirms, in real time and on-chain, that the collateral exists and is fully segregated. The retailer carries zero credit risk on the client from the moment the agreement begins.

The retailer does not need to understand tokenisation. It needs to understand three things: it earns more per customer, it risks nothing on payment default, and it changes nothing about how it procures or delivers energy. That is why the retailer signs.

## PART IV - HOW IT WORKS

### 10. The Protocol - The Physical Tether

#### The BlackSlon Settlement Zone
The BlackSlon Settlement Zone is a governed price corridor established independently for every power and gas market. It is the structural mechanism that ensures virtual token prices operate within bounds that physical market reality defines - not through regulatory mandate, but through mathematical constraint enforced at smart contract level on Arbitrum.

The corridor is anchored to the Settlement Anchor - a weighted, smoothed reference price calculated from physical hub data across four time horizons: spot, front month, front quarter, and calendar year, combined in fixed proportions that prevent any single segment from dominating. The Settlement Anchor is further stabilised by a three-day recursive filter that prevents a single anomalous session from shifting the corridor discontinuously.

From the Settlement Anchor, the corridor extends asymmetrically: ten percent below as the floor, twenty percent above as the ceiling. The asymmetry reflects a structural truth - energy prices spike upward far more violently than they collapse. The wider ceiling accommodates genuine directional moves. The tighter floor protects long-term token holders from irrational single-session collapses that the physical market corrects within days.

All orders outside this corridor are rejected at submission. Token prices move freely within it - determined entirely by supply and demand on the Order Book. The protocol has no operational capacity outside the zone - by design.

Full mathematical specification: Technical Specification document.

#### The BlackSlon Energy Settlement Index
The BSEI is the transaction-derived settlement benchmark - computed exclusively from executed BS-P/G trades on the Open Order Book. It is the single source of data for Mark-to-Market valuation, margin requirements, and settlement integrity across all BlackSlon markets.

The BSEI is published continuously, on-chain, free of charge. Full mathematical specification: Technical Specification document.

#### BSR - The Protocol Reserve Token
BSR is the native reserve token of the BlackSlon Protocol. It is not a stablecoin. It is not a redemption instrument. It is an investment in the protocol itself - an asset whose value appreciates as the ecosystem grows, through a deflationary burn mechanism funded by all ecosystem revenue.

Every Protocol transaction generates fees. 85 percent flows to the Protocol Vault. When the Vault holds a verified surplus over all outstanding obligations, the burn mechanism activates - permanently destroying a governance-set percentage of BSR from circulation. As supply shrinks and the Vault grows, the value of each remaining BSR appreciates. Holders do not receive dividends. They hold an asset whose scarcity increases proportionally to ecosystem success.

The short selling of BSR is permanently prohibited at smart contract level - an immutable constraint that cannot be overridden by governance.

BSR also functions as trading collateral - the higher the BSR ratio in a participant's vault, the lower the transaction fee:

### 11. The Liquidity Architecture

**Layer 1 - The Open Order Book.** The primary price discovery mechanism. Participants trade BS-P and BS-G tokens directly with each other at market-driven prices within the BSSZ corridor. Strict Price-Time Priority. No preferential routing. No hidden tiers. 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.

**Layer 2 - The Prime Broker.** A regulated financial institution of institutional standing serves two functions simultaneously.

**As market maker,** the prime broker's energy trading desk continuously posts two-sided quotes on the Order Book:
- BID: BSEI minus agreed spread (typically 2-3%)
- ASK: BSEI plus agreed spread (typically 2-3%)

All quotes calibrated against real-time prices on physical energy markets (TTF, Phelix, NBP, PSV) and financial derivative markets. All quotes within the active BSSZ corridor. The prime broker earns the bid-ask spread on every executed transaction. Because the Prime Broker Mandate Manager provides real-time visibility into the Protocol's aggregate flow, the prime broker can manage its inventory with an informational advantage unavailable to any other participant - making market making in BlackSlon commercially attractive beyond contractual obligation.

**As financial guarantor,** the prime broker maintains a hedging position in regulated energy derivative markets - sized to offset the Protocol's aggregate net delta at all times. When a participant closes a profitable position, settlement occurs directly between the Protocol and the prime broker at the prevailing market price per the Prime Brokerage Agreement. The Vault is replenished by the prime broker's direct settlement obligation.

A critical regulatory property of BS-P and BS-G tokens determines the entire capital architecture. Under MiCA, Asset-Referenced Tokens require 1:1 reserves against every token in circulation - locking capital that cannot be deployed. BS-P and BS-G tokens are Utility Tokens under MiCA Title II. The Protocol is not required to hold 1:1 reserves. Instead: participant collateral, Protocol Vault surplus, prime broker hedge, and BSR Stability Reserve provide full economic backing for every outstanding position - without locking idle capital. The difference between what participants post as Initial Margin and what the prime broker requires to hedge the same exposure remains in the Vault as productive capital - funding the BSR burn mechanism.

**Layer 3 - The Protocol Vault.** Market maker of absolute last resort. Activates only when no organic counterparty exists and the prime broker has not quoted - structurally rare under normal conditions. Hard-capped at 15 percent of total Vault value. Inactive when H_solv is in Tier III or worse.

### 12. Risk Architecture - Intelligent, Not Punitive

Traditional clearing systems treat liquidation as a binary event - positions closed in full, at unfavourable prices, with no regard for capital preservation. Statistical data from retail trading platforms shows that 70-80 percent of participants lose capital - not because their market view was wrong, but because intraday volatility forces liquidation before the thesis plays out. A correct thesis, liquidated too early, is indistinguishable from a wrong one.

BlackSlon rejects this architecture entirely. The risk framework operates on a different principle: proportional, surgical response designed to preserve as much of a participant's position as possible while maintaining the Protocol's solvency obligations.

Two independent risk engines operate simultaneously - one at participant level, one at protocol level. They share no data except aggregate statistics. They have no authority over each other. They serve different objectives.

#### H_user: Individual Health Monitoring
H_user calculates, monitors, and acts upon the Health Factor of every individual participant position in real time. It is architecturally distinct from the ISA because it must sometimes act against the participant's immediate preference - reducing positions the participant would prefer to keep - in order to protect both the participant's remaining capital and the Protocol's collateral integrity.

The ISA cannot delay or override any H_user response. H_user operates independently of participant instruction once the Intervention Zone is breached.

**Health Factor Formula:**
```
H_user = Equity_total / (Initial_Margin_total × 0.5)
```

**Health Zones and Automated Responses:**

| Zone | H_user | Protocol Response |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| SAFE | > 1.10 | Full operational access. No restrictions. |
| WARNING | 1.05-1.10 | ISA notification. No automatic action. |
| RESTRICTED | 1.00-1.05 | Reduce-Only. No new positions. |
| INTERVENTION | 1.00 | Smart Incremental Liquidation activates. |

#### Smart Incremental Liquidation - SIL
SIL is the mechanism that replaces catastrophic forced closure with surgical, proportional position reduction.

**Activation trigger:** H_user 1.00

**Recovery target:** H_user 1.00 + 0.02

**Algorithm:**
```
FUNCTION SmartIncrementalLiquidation(account):

  LOOP:
    positions = GetOpenPositions(account)
    candidates = []

    FOR EACH position j IN positions:
      h_after = SimulateHealthFactor(account, j, 0.10)
      loss = SimulateLoss(position_j, 0.10)
      candidates.append({position: j,
                         h_after: h_after,
                         loss: loss})

    # Sort ascending by loss - smallest loss first
    candidates.sort(by=loss, ascending=True)

    FOR EACH candidate IN candidates:
      IF candidate.h_after >= 1.00 + epsilon:
        ExecutePartialClose(candidate.position, 0.10)
        SettleLoss_50_50(account, candidate.loss)
        RETURN  # Health restored - stop

    # No single 10% cut sufficient
    best = candidates[0]
    ExecutePartialClose(best.position, 0.10)
    SettleLoss_50_50(account, best.loss)

    IF no positions remain: BREAK

FUNCTION SettleLoss_50_50(account, loss_amount):
  bsr_deduction = (loss_amount * 0.50) / P_BSR_current
  euroe_deduction = loss_amount * 0.50
  account.bsr_balance -= bsr_deduction
  account.euroe_balance -= euroe_deduction
  Vault.eEURO += euroe_deduction
  Vault.BSR += bsr_deduction
  # Transferred to Vault - NOT sold on open market
  # Prevents cascading price impact
```

**Key properties of SIL:**
- Always closes the least costly position first
- Always in 10% increments - never more than necessary
- Stops immediately when Health Factor is restored
- Losses settled 50% eEURO / 50% BSR - maintains collateral profile
- Seized collateral transferred to Vault - never sold on open market during liquidation event

#### H_solv: Protocol Solvency Monitoring
H_solv monitors the aggregate capital adequacy of the entire Protocol continuously. It answers one question at all times: can the Protocol honour every outstanding obligation to every participant simultaneously?

H_solv is the highest authority in the Protocol's AI architecture. Its Tier responses override all other AI modules and all participant-level activity. No module and no participant can override or delay H_solv's systemic responses.

**H_solv Core Formula:**
```
H_solv_CORE = (V_eEURO + BSR-SR_balance) / (|PnL_ITM| + IM_total + Reserve_Op)
```

**The Five Tiers:**

| Tier | H_solv_CORE | Name | Automated Response |
|------|-------------|------|-------------------|
| I | > 1.15 | Expansion | Full operations. Burn active. |
| II | 1.10-1.15 | Equilibrium | Enhanced monitoring. |
| III | 1.05-1.10 | Mitigation | New positions suspended. BSR-SR T1 prepared. |
| IV | 1.00-1.05 | Alert | Reduce-Only. BSR-SR T2 deployed. |
| V | < 1.00 | Safeguard | Full stop. BSR-SR T3. ADS Lock if P_BSR -10%. Governance vote 24h. |

**Anti-Death-Spiral Lock**
Trigger conditions - both must be met simultaneously:
- Condition 1: H_solv_CORE < 1.00 (Tier V)
- Condition 2: P_BSR -10% within any 24-hour period

**Effect:**
- BSR collateral frozen at T-24h price for all H_user and H_solv calculations
- SIL cannot use BSR depreciation as trigger for further liquidations
- Maximum lock duration: 48 hours
- Early release: if H_user average > 1.10 for 60 consecutive minutes

**Purpose:** Prevents the reflexivity cascade where falling BSR price reduces collateral values triggers H_user liquidations liquidated BSR sold into market further BSR price decline more liquidations. The lock breaks this feedback loop by decoupling BSR market price from collateral valuation during the acute stress period.

### 14. ISA - Your Energy Companion

The ISA is the participant-facing intelligence layer. Its scope is strictly bounded to the individual participant it serves - their vault, their open positions, current BSEI values, current BSSZ corridor parameters, and their configured automation rules.

The ISA receives Health Factor alerts from H_user through a read-only interface. It can relay these alerts to the participant and surface response options. It cannot influence H_user's assessment and cannot delay H_user's automated responses. When H_solv restricts new position openings globally, the ISA's DCA and dip-response rules that would open new positions are suspended - enforced by the smart contract, not by ISA-level logic.

The ISA has no access to H_solv data beyond the current Tier status published on-chain. It has no access to aggregate participant data, prime broker positions, or Physical Meridian inputs. It cannot influence any Layer Zero or Layer One module in any way.

**ISA Functions:**

**Advisory:**

- Exit trigger: close position if BSEI rises Z%

**Account Abstraction Integration:**
The ISA operates through ERC-4337 Smart Accounts with pre-approved session keys. When a participant configures an automation rule, the ISA receives a session key that allows it to execute specific operations within defined limits without requiring manual signature for each execution. The participant can revoke the session key at any time, immediately stopping all ISA automation.

**Regulatory Position:**
ISA operates as execution layer for client-defined rules - not as a discretionary portfolio manager. This architectural boundary allows ISA to operate without a MiFID II portfolio management licence.

**PERMITTED (without MiFID II licence):**

**NOT PERMITTED (would require licence):**
- Discretionary investment decisions
- Managing assets without explicit instruction
- Overriding participant-defined parameters

Every automation rule: explicitly defined by participant, explicitly approved at configuration time, executable only within set parameters, logged and notified every execution, revocable at any time.

### 16. Regulatory Framework

BlackSlon operates at the intersection of two regulated domains: crypto-asset services (MiCA) and wholesale energy markets (REMIT). The regulatory position of each instrument and activity is defined with precision below.

**Core regulatory thesis:** BS-P and BS-G tokens are utility tokens under MiCA Title II. They are not asset-referenced tokens, not e-money tokens, and not financial instruments under MiFID II. They are not wholesale energy products under REMIT because they lack the defining characteristics of REMIT-regulated instruments: no delivery point, no delivery date, no delivery period. REMIT obligations arise only at the moment of V2P conversion into a physical supply contract - and then apply to the supply contract, not to the token.

**Primary regulatory filing:** CASP licence application with the Bank of Lithuania under MiCA Article 59. Single EU licence provides passport to operate in all 27 EU member states for crypto-asset services. Energy supply licences obtained jurisdiction by jurisdiction as a separate regulatory track.

**MiCA Classification:**
- **BSR:** Utility Token (Title II) - does not maintain stable value, value derived from Protocol Vault performance
- **BS-P and BS-G:** Utility Tokens (Title II) - provide access to Protocol services, price moves freely within BSSZ corridor
- **eEURO:** Electronic Money Token (Title IV) - issued by regulated third parties (Monerium/Circle), used as settlement currency

**REMIT Analysis:**
BS-P and BS-G tokens are not wholesale energy products under REMIT Article 2(4):
- Not supply contracts: no delivery point, no delivery period, no volume profile
- Not derivatives: no fixed settlement date, no notional amount in reference commodity
- Not large supply contracts: do not represent supply to final customers

REMIT obligations apply only to physical supply contracts created through V2P conversion - not to the tokens themselves.
