# ISA Execution Architecture & Custodial Security Model

---

## The Execution Problem in Web3

Every protocol that offers automated execution on behalf of users confronts the same fundamental tension. Web3 is built on self-sovereignty - the principle that only the holder of a private key can authorise movement of assets associated with that key. This is the source of Web3's trustlessness and its primary security guarantee. It is also, for most retail participants, an insurmountable operational barrier.

A household energy buyer in Lisbon does not manage a hardware wallet. A small business owner in Warsaw does not understand session keys or transaction signing. A manufacturing company's finance departments does not want its treasury operations dependent on a MetaMask browser extension. For these participants - who represent the overwhelming majority of the Volume Seeker class that BlackSlon Protocol is designed to serve - self-custody is not a feature. It is an obstacle.

The ISA's automated execution layer requires a model that allows intelligent, rule-based portfolio actions to occur without demanding that the participant sign every individual transaction. The BlackSlon Protocol resolves this through a custodial architecture - Mode 3 - that delivers the simplicity of a banking application without sacrificing the transparency and verifiability of on-chain settlement.

---

## Mode 3: The Custodial Vault

Under the custodial model, BlackSlon Protocol holds the participant's Reserve Vault assets on their behalf. The participant does not manage a private key. They do not interact with a browser wallet. They access their account through a standard authenticated interface - email address, password, and two-factor authentication - and the protocol's infrastructure handles all on-chain transactions in the background.

This is not a new model. It is the model used by every major centralised exchange, every digital bank, and every regulated financial institution that offers crypto asset services to retail clients. Coinbase holds assets on behalf of its users. Revolut holds fiat and crypto on behalf of its users. The participant trusts the institution to custody their assets safely and to act only in accordance with their instructions. In exchange, they receive an experience that is indistinguishable from a conventional financial application.

What makes the BlackSlon custodial model different from a conventional exchange custody arrangement is the on-chain transparency that surrounds it. The protocol's Proof of Reserve publishes the aggregate collateral backing all outstanding tokens in real time, verified by a decentralised oracle network. Participants cannot verify their individual vault holdings on-chain the way a self-custody user can - but they can verify that the protocol's total reserve is fully collateralised at every moment. The systemic guarantee is on-chain even when the individual custody relationship is not.

## Authentication and Access

Participant access to the BlackSlon interface is governed by a three-factor authentication model that reflects the sensitivity of energy settlement positions held over multi-year horizons.

The first factor is the participant's email address and password, stored with industry-standard hashing and salting. Passwords are never stored in recoverable form. The protocol enforces minimum complexity requirements and flags credential reuse against known breach databases at registration.

The second factor is a time-based one-time password generated by an authenticator application - Google Authenticator, Authy, or any TOTP-compatible application. SMS-based two-factor authentication is not offered, reflecting the well-documented vulnerability of SMS delivery to SIM-swapping attacks. For participants who prefer hardware security, FIDO2-compatible hardware tokens - YubiKey and equivalents - are supported as an alternative second factor.

The third factor, applied selectively to high-value or unusual actions, is a confirmation step that requires the participant to acknowledge the specific action being taken before it is executed. Withdrawals above a defined threshold, changes to ISA automation rules, and modifications to collateral allocation all trigger this third factor regardless of whether the session is already authenticated. A participant whose session has been compromised cannot have funds withdrawn without this explicit per-action confirmation.

Session duration is limited. Inactive sessions expire after a defined period. New sessions from unrecognised devices or IP addresses trigger an additional verification step and a notification to the participant's registered email address. Participants can view all active sessions and terminate any of them instantly from within the interface.

## What the ISA Can Do Without Active Participation

Once a participant has configured their ISA automation rules and authenticated their session, the ISA can execute the following actions without requiring the participant to log in or confirm each individual transaction.

**Scheduled DCA purchases** execute at the configured time and amount, provided the participant's vault contains sufficient eEURO or BSR to fund the purchase and the order book price is within the participant's configured maximum. If the price exceeds the maximum, the purchase is skipped and the participant is notified.

**Dip response purchases** execute when the configured price threshold is met, funded from the pre-allocated reserve the participant designated for this purpose at configuration time. The dip reserve is a separate allocation within the vault - the ISA cannot draw on the participant's general vault balance beyond what was explicitly designated for dip response activity.

**Health Factor maintenance transfers** execute when the Health Factor falls below the participant's configured threshold, moving eEURO from a pre-funded maintenance reserve into the active collateral position. Again, the maintenance reserve is separately allocated and explicitly capped at configuration time.

**Collateral rebalancing** executes when the actual BSR to eEURO ratio drifts beyond the participant's configured tolerance bands, purchasing the underweight asset from the order book using available vault liquidity up to a defined monthly rebalancing budget.

In every case, the ISA acts within boundaries that the participant defined and approved at configuration time. The ISA cannot exceed those boundaries. It cannot redirect funds to external addresses. It cannot change the participant's collateral ratio beyond the rebalancing rules. It cannot open new positions beyond the DCA and dip response programmes. It cannot close existing positions without the participant's explicit instruction.

## What the ISA Cannot Do Under Any Circumstances

The custodial model creates a surface area of trust that must be bounded explicitly. The following actions are architecturally impossible for the ISA regardless of the participant's configuration, regardless of instructions from any source, and regardless of market conditions.

**The ISA cannot withdraw assets to any external address** - on-chain wallet, exchange deposit address, or bank account - without an explicit withdrawal request initiated by the authenticated participant in the current session, confirmed through the third-factor per-action verification, and subject to a mandatory processing delay of 24 hours during which the participant can cancel.

**The ISA cannot modify the participant's authentication credentials**, registered email address, or two-factor authentication configuration. Changes to account security parameters require re-authentication from a registered device and a verification link sent to the existing registered email address.

**The ISA cannot increase the scope of its own automation rules.** If a participant configured a DCA rule capped at 500 EUR per month, the ISA cannot increase that cap to 1,000 EUR even if the participant's vault balance would support it. Scope increases require the participant to log in, navigate to the ISA configuration interface, and explicitly approve the change.

**The ISA cannot act on instructions received through any channel other than the authenticated participant interface.** Instructions embedded in emails, support chat messages, or third-party applications are not recognised as valid by the ISA execution layer. The only valid instruction source is the authenticated session within the BlackSlon interface or, for institutional participants, the authenticated API connection with the participant's pre-registered API credentials.

## The Custodial Obligation

By accepting custody of participant assets, BlackSlon Protocol accepts a legal and operational obligation that extends beyond the technical architecture described above.

**Segregation of assets:** Participant assets held in custody are segregated from BlackSlon's operating capital. The protocol maintains a strict separation between assets held on behalf of participants - Reserve Vault balances, collateral positions, and cash equivalents pending settlement - and the protocol's own treasury, fee revenue, and operational reserves. This segregation is maintained at the accounting level, the banking level, and the on-chain level. In the event of BlackSlon's insolvency, participant assets are not available to satisfy the protocol's creditors.

**Annual independent audit:** The protocol undergoes annual independent audit of its custodial practices, including verification that participant assets are held in full and that segregation requirements are maintained. Audit reports are published in full within 30 days of completion.

**Professional indemnity insurance:** The protocol maintains professional indemnity insurance and cyber liability insurance at levels appropriate to the aggregate value of assets under custody. Coverage levels are disclosed to participants on request and updated as assets under custody grow.

**Regulatory authorisation:** The custodial model requires authorisation as a Crypto Asset Service Provider under MiCA - specifically, the custody and administration of crypto assets on behalf of clients. BlackSlon Protocol holds this authorisation as a prerequisite for operating the custodial vault. The authorisation number, the supervising national competent authority, and the scope of the authorisation are disclosed on the protocol's regulatory information page and verified through the ESMA register of authorised CASPs.

## The Participant Experience

From the participant's perspective, the custodial model and the ISA automation layer together produce an experience that is closer to a premium digital banking application than to a crypto protocol.

Onboarding takes minutes. The participant provides their email address, creates a password, configures two-factor authentication, and completes the identity verification required under MiCA. They deposit eEURO or BSR into their vault through a bank transfer or crypto on-ramp, depending on their preference and technical familiarity.

They configure their ISA rules through a guided interface that translates their objectives into automation parameters. The interface presents each rule in plain language before the participant approves it, confirms the maximum monthly expenditure that the rule can trigger, and provides a clear explanation of the conditions under which each rule activates.

After that, the participant can engage with the interface as frequently or infrequently as they choose. The ISA monitors their position, executes their rules, and sends notifications when their attention is genuinely required - Health Factor warnings, large market movements, rule execution confirmations, and monthly position summaries. The participant who wants to check their portfolio daily can do so. The participant who wants to configure their rules once and review quarterly can do that too. The protocol accommodates both.

When the participant wants to withdraw - at the end of a supply agreement, when a trading position is closed, or for any other reason - they initiate the withdrawal through the authenticated interface, confirm through the per-action verification step, and receive their funds within the 24-hour processing window. The processing delay is a security feature, not an operational limitation. It is the window during which a participant who discovers that their account has been compromised can cancel a withdrawal they did not initiate.

## Why Custodial Is the Right Choice for BlackSlon's Participant Base

The participants that BlackSlon Protocol is designed to serve - households securing their energy costs, small and medium businesses managing procurement, corporate treasury desks hedging multi-year supply obligations, and energy retailers settling client contracts - are not, in the main, sophisticated Web3 users. They are energy market participants who have been given access to a financial instrument that previously required institutional infrastructure to access.

Asking these participants to manage hardware wallets, understand session key delegation, or navigate the complexities of smart contract vault architecture would exclude the majority of them from the protocol before they ever placed their first order. The custodial model removes that barrier entirely. It asks participants to do one thing: trust that BlackSlon Protocol holds their assets safely and executes their instructions faithfully. In exchange, it accepts the legal, operational, and regulatory obligations that this trust demands.

The on-chain Proof of Reserve is the mechanism through which that trust is made verifiable rather than merely asserted. Participants may not be able to verify their individual position on-chain. But they can verify, at any moment, that the protocol holds sufficient reserves to honour every outstanding position across every participant simultaneously. The systemic guarantee is public and mathematical. The custodial relationship is private and contractual. Together, they provide the foundation on which a retail energy settlement protocol can be built.

The custodial model is not a compromise. It is the correct architecture for the participant base BlackSlon Protocol serves - and the ISA is the intelligence layer that makes it not just accessible, but genuinely valuable to every participant who uses it.
